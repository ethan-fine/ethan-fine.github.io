A little bit about my background...

I first became interested in programming when I was eleven years old. I was fascinated with the idea of a digital world of endless possibilities, where one had the ultimate verdict in every case. It had ocurred to me that if, while others claim otherwise, not everything was possible in the world we live in, it had to be possible somewhere else. Programming offered such solution.

At the time, I was very interested in the popular 'sand-box type' game, Minecraft, which was another world in which there were no limits, physically at least... I began wanting to edit the game, and develop new integrations into the game. I began by creating simple plugins for the multi-player server I was working on, HungerCrafters, as I could not afford to hire other developers. I knew the only option to succeed was to become the developer.

Eventually, my skill level in Java and with the Bukkit API had elevated, and the server which had started up with a $15 dollar investment began hitting 100 constant players, and making around $3,000 dollars a month. All in all, I made around $50,000 dollars from HungerCrafters, at thirteen years old. Unfortunately, the server did end up dying out after a while, as I began developing for other servers, with a paid position.

Numerous attempts at recreating a stable (community-wise) and engaged server failed in the following years, so I remained with an elevated pay, for fourteen years old, of $20 dollars an hour at a Minecraft server called MeepCraft. After a while, my ambition began to kick in once again, and I sought a new world of possibilities. Possibly, a world which had a bigger name, a world open to over 1 billion devices, iOS. Almost at the same time, I exposed myself to an even bigger audience, approximately 60% of the world's population, the web...
